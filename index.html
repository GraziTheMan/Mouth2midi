Mouth2MIDI — quick guide

Transport

Start opens the mic and begins listening. Record captures the notes into a take. Export .mid opens the share sheet to send the MIDI file to Cubasis, Files, etc.

The sheet

The scrolling roll shows the green pitch trace (what it hears) and the purple note blocks (what gets recorded). The amber dashed lines are your range band; lanes in your scale are shaded.

Pitch & notes

Scale / Root — detected pitches snap to this scale. Chromatic = no snapping.

Gate — input-loudness threshold. Below it, sound is treated as silence. Raise it to ignore breath/room noise; lower it if quiet notes are missed. Watch the input-level bar.

Confidence — how sure the pitch detector must be before trusting a note. Higher = fewer wrong/octave notes but drops uncertain (often low or breathy) notes. Lower = more sensitive.

Capture — how close to a note's exact pitch you must be for it to register (like a tuner's tolerance). Wide (±50¢) = forgiving; narrow (±10¢) = you must hit it. Also controls how "sticky" a held note is.

Range low / high — only pitches inside this band make notes; everything else is ignored. Set it to your comfortable singing range.

Timing

Tempo — the BPM written into the exported file; set it to your project's tempo. Quantize — snaps exported note timing to a grid (1/4, 1/8, 1/16) so it lands on the beat in your DAW. Metronome — On gives a visual + haptic beat and a one-bar count-in before recording (no audio, so it won't bleed into the mic).

Calibrate to my voice

Hum your lowest and highest comfortable notes and stay silent for a moment; the app sets your range band and noise gate to match your voice. Press Start first so the mic is live.

Beatbox & Calibrate my drums

Set Engine → Beatbox to turn kicks/snares/hi-hats into GM drum notes (channel 10). Because everyone's mouth sounds sit differently, Calibrate my drums learns your three sounds: tap out each one a few times when prompted and every later hit snaps to whichever of your sounds it's closest to. Runs entirely on-device; Reset returns to the built-in detector.
